Deep Drain Cleaning

The Most Thorough Drain Cleaning Method

A standard drain snake breaks through a clog and restores some flow, but it leaves grease, scale, and residue coating the pipe walls. Those residues catch new buildup quickly, leading to the same blockage problem weeks or months later. High-pressure hydro jetting in Chadron, NE uses water at precisely controlled pressure to blast the interior walls of the pipe clean, removing grease layers, mineral deposits, root fragments, and debris completely.

The result is a drain that not only flows freely but stays clear longer because there is nothing for new buildup to cling to. Hydro jetting is particularly effective for restaurant and commercial kitchens dealing with heavy grease accumulation, for main sewer lines with root problems, and for any residential line with a history of recurring blockages. We conduct a camera inspection before jetting to confirm the pipe condition and after to verify the result.

Key Advantages

Why Hydro Jetting Outperforms Snaking

Complete Pipe Wall Cleaning

Hydro jetting scours the full interior circumference of the pipe, not just the center channel. This removes grease layers and mineral deposits that accumulate and cause recurring clogs.

Longer-Lasting Results

Because the pipe walls are clean after hydro jetting, new debris has nothing to stick to. Most customers see significantly fewer recurring blockages compared to traditional cable cleaning.

Safe & Chemical-Free

Hydro jetting uses only high-pressure water — no harsh chemicals that can corrode pipes, damage drain fields, or require special disposal. It is environmentally responsible and safe for all connected systems.

FAQs

Common Questions About Hydro Jetting

A drain snake creates an opening through a clog but leaves pipe walls coated with residue. Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to scour the complete pipe interior, removing grease, scale, root fragments, and accumulated debris. The result is a genuinely clean pipe with restored full-diameter flow.

Hydro jetting is safe for most pipe materials and ages when performed by a trained technician at the appropriate pressure setting. For older or potentially fragile pipes, we conduct a camera inspection first to assess condition before deciding whether jetting is appropriate or whether a lower-pressure method is better.

Standard drain cleaning works well for a simple blockage. Hydro jetting is the better choice when you have recurring clogs, heavy grease accumulation in a commercial kitchen, root intrusions in the sewer line, or a main line with years of buildup that standard methods cannot fully address.
